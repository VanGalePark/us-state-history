Alabama

The state of Alabama was named after the Alabama river. That river was named by European exporers after a Native American tribe that lived in the territory and first appeared in 1540, the Alibamu Tribe. The name Alabama is thought to be a combination of two Choctaw words; Alba and Amo. "Alba" would mean vegetation, herbs, or plants. "Amo" would mean gatherer or picker. So together they might mean Vegetation Gatherers, which would be a fitting description for the Alibamu Tribe who cleared a lot of land for agricultural purposes.

The United States Congress created the Alabama Territory on March 3rd, 1817, then it became the 22nd state two years later on December 14th, 1819.
