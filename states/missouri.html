Missouri

On August 10th, 1821 Missouri became the 24th state as a part of the Missouri Compromise. Missouri was the first state that was entirely on the west of the Mississippi River to be admitted into the Union. Missouri applied for statehood in December of 1818 as a slave state. The Missouri Compromise cleared the way for Missouri's acceptance into the Union as a slave state, alongside Maine as a free state, to preserve the balance. The Missouri Compromise also stated that the remaining portion of the Louisiana Territory above the 36°30′ line would be free from slavery.
